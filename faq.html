FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I bring with me?

The most important thing is plenty of water, you will need it! You do not have to bring any equipment as we have everything you need to train with. If you have you own pads or gloves however you may wish to bring them.

Do I need any previous experience?

Not at all, most new members have no previous experience in martial arts. You will be guided through every technique so that you can progress quickly and safely.

Do I need to be fit and flexible?

You will be at an advantage if you already have some degree of fitness and flexibility but it is not essential. As part of our training we work on both and you will find that you improve very quickly.

Will I be the only woman there?

Don’t worry there are other women in the club so there will be others at practically every class.

Will I get hurt?

We take our students wellbeing very seriously and have tailored our classes to ensure that you are properly warmed up and stretched, and your joints are strengthened before being put under too much strain. In any martial arts training there is some risk of injury but through proper technique and care this can be minimised. You will likely feel aches from using muscles that are not used to being exercised however.

When can I train with weapons?

After you have learnt the first two forms you will be able to start using weapons. This is to ensure that you have a proper grasp of the basic skills, reducing the chance that you might hurt yourself.

Is this an effective form of self-defence?

Absolutely! Practically everything we do has self defence applications.

Can I join if I am not a student at the University of Southampton?

Yes you can, everyone is welcome at the club.
