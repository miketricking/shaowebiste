The Shaolin Temple is a Buddhist monastery, originally built on Songshan in 495 AD. Monks at Shaolin have practiced Chan Buddhism and martial arts for nearly its entire history. The most often told story for the origin of Shaolin Kung Fu is that the Indian monk Bodhidarma came to Shaolin in 527 AD and upon finding the monks too physically weak to practice long periods of meditation introduced a series of physical exercises, variously said to be based on animal and farming movements. However, there are also records which state that some of the earliest monks were expert martial artists, perhaps indicating a former military life. Throughout the temple’s history it has been a refuge for many former military personnel, leading to a continual influx of new martial skills.


During the Sui dynasty (581-618) Shaolin Kung Fu was formed into its own distinct system which has developed continually ever since. Throughout their history Shaolin monks have been involved in combat, defending the temple against bandits and at times being caught up in political conflicts. The long association of Chan Buddhism and martial arts at the temple has led to Shaolin Kung Fu forming an essential part of Buddhist practice there. The practice of kung fu is often regarded as a form of moving meditation.


Throughout its history the Shaolin Temple has been destroyed and rebuilt many times, at some points remaining derelict for some time. More recently it was burned in 1928, destroying a significant portion of the temple, and the monks were subjected to persecution during the Cultural Revolution. During these periods the Shaolin monks lived amongst the communities of Songshan, helping to further spread Shaolin Kung Fu throughout the area.


In the 1980’s the rebuilding of the temple began, with many monks returning. During this time the popularity of Shaolin Kung Fu grew and it became internationally recognised for its mix of traditional forms and acrobatics.

Info

Shaolin is also well known for its practitioner’s fitness, flexibility and body conditioning; our training has been designed to allow quick progress in these areas whilst ensuring safe and balanced development. Whilst many exercise routines focus almost entirely on the larger muscles, we include strengthening of the joints matched with carefully planned introduction of techniques to avoid injury.
Shaolin Kung Fu’s long history has seen it influenced by many martial arts practitioners from all over China, making it a vast system of self-defence. Our syllabus is therefore wide ranging and includes; traditional fist and weapon forms, each of which is its own sub-style or part of a sub-style, containing many practical applications; acrobatics which the Shaolin monks are famous for; Chin Na, which covers grappling and joint locks and Sanda, also known as Chinese kick boxing.
Shaolin Kung Fu originates from the Shaolin Temple in Henan, China. Whilst there are many styles of martial arts which make use of the Shaolin name, we train in the style practiced in the temple by the Shaolin monks. The almost unique experience of our instructors allows us to incorporate aspects of traditional Shaolin Kung Fu practiced by folk masters on Songshan mountain which are no longer practiced in the temple.

Meet the members

Shifu Yan Xin - Club Founder

Shifu Yan Xin is a 34th generation master from the Shaolin Temple. Shifu Yan Xin started training in Shaolin Kung Fu at the age of eight, becoming a master at nineteen. In 1999 he led the Wheel of Life tour and in 2000 performed with Jackie Chan at the Bollywood Film Awards. He then settled in England to teach kung fu and spread Shaolin culture, sharing his passion with students across the UK. He also teaches in Ireland and runs the online learning platform KungFu.Life. Shifu Yan Xin runs seminars at the club throughout the year.

Josh Viney - Club Co-founder

Josh was amongst Shifu Yan Xin’s first students in England and helped establish the Southampton Shaolin Temple Kung Fu club, having previously trained in Wing Chun. For more than a decade Josh has spent much of his year training in China, initially at the famous kung fu school Tagou and then with folk masters around Songshan. When in the UK he runs classes at the club.

Damien Campbell-Bell - Da Ge

Damien has been training in Shaolin Kung Fu for over eight years, after dabbling in a number of different martial arts. In 2013 he won first place in a regional Shaolin forms competition. He has been teaching for the club for more than five years and is one of the principle instructors.

Chris Challis - Da Ge

Chris has been training in Shaolin Kung Fu for over nine years. Prior to this he trained in Tae Kwon Do for thirteen years, attaining a second degree black belt and winning a number of national tournaments. In 2011 he won second and third place in categories at the Team Shaolin European Championship. Chris has been teaching for the club for more than five years and is one of the principle instructors.

Neil Hamilton-Fairley – Zhu Jiao

Neil has been training in Shaolin Kung Fu for over four years. He started training Aikido at the same time and formerly practiced Kickboxing for 8 years, attaining a second dan black belt. Neil assists in teaching and takes classes when other instructors are not available.

Tom Hunt – Zhu Jiao

Tom has been training in Shaolin Kung Fu for over three years, having formerly trained in Wado-Ryu Karate for two years. He assists in teaching and takes classes when other instructors are not available.
